Amaka James

14 years old

21 March,2025

Amara was only fourteen when her village was struck by a violent terrorist attack. In the chaos, she was separated from her family and left with injuries that would take months to heal. Yet today, she stands as a symbol of quiet strength—rebuilding her life through counseling, education support, and a community of survivors who refuse to give up hope. Her journey reminds us that behind every crisis is a resilient human spirit.

Full Story

Amara was only 14 when her village in northern Nigeria was attacked at night. She remembers the sound of doors breaking, the rush of people running, and the sudden darkness filled with smoke. In the chaos, she was separated from her mother and younger brother. Amara survived with injuries to her leg and severe emotional trauma. For weeks she could not speak, often waking in tears from nightmares. Through the NGO’s trauma-healing center, she began counseling sessions, received medical support, and was placed in a safe learning program. Today, Amara is back in school and dreams of becoming a nurse. She says, “I survived, but healing didn’t start until someone cared enough to help me find my voice again.”

Grace Chima

26 years old

13 August 2025

Grace escaped a violent attack, but the emotional wounds remained long after the chaos ended. She battled anxiety, nightmares, and a constant sense of fear that affected her daily life and education. Through trauma counseling, peer support groups, and mental-health resources provided by the NGO, she gradually learned to regain control and trust again. Today, Grace advocates for emotional healing and encourages others to seek help. Her recovery shows that invisible wounds can heal.

Full Story

Grace was seventeen when violence struck her community. Though she escaped without physical injuries, the emotional impact followed her for months. She struggled with constant fear, sudden panic attacks, and recurring nightmares that made sleep almost impossible. Her grades dropped, and she withdrew from friends because she didn’t know how to explain what she was feeling. Through the NGO’s trauma-healing program, Grace received counseling, attended group therapy, and learned coping tools to manage anxiety. With time, she regained confidence, returned fully to school, and found comfort in connecting with others who had lived through similar experiences. Grace says, “I didn’t look hurt, but inside I was breaking. Counseling helped me put the pieces back together.”

Daniel Effiong

23 years old

14 June,2025

Daniel survived a violent attack that destroyed his home, farm, and sense of security. Forced to flee with his young family, he struggled to provide even basic needs. Through emergency relief, counseling, and a skills-training program, he slowly rebuilt stability and hope. Today, Daniel earns income as a carpenter and supports other displaced families. His journey reflects resilience, strength, and the power of restoring dignity after loss. His story continues to inspire many survivors.

Full Story

Daniel was a farmer and church youth leader. One early morning, his community was attacked, forcing families to flee into the nearby forest. Daniel helped guide several children to safety, but by the time the attack ended, his home and farm had been burned to the ground. With no shelter, food, or source of income, Daniel struggled to support his wife and newborn child. The NGO provided emergency food assistance, temporary shelter materials, and agricultural support for displaced farmers. Daniel later joined a skills-training program where he learned carpentry, which now helps him earn a steady income for his family. He shares, “I lost everything I owned, but not my will to stand again.”

Naomi Grace

37 years old

11 September,2025

Naomi lost her husband during a violent attack that forced her to flee with her three children. Grieving and overwhelmed, she struggled to meet even their most basic needs. Through the NGO’s emergency support, trauma counseling, and a skills-empowerment program, Naomi learned tailoring and began rebuilding her life. Today, she runs a small business that sustains her family. Her strength reminds us that healing is possible when compassion and opportunity come together for many.

Full Story

Naomi, a widowed mother of three, lost her husband during an attack on their village. Left alone, she struggled to protect her children and provide for them. The NGO helped Naomi access trauma counseling, food relief, and later enrolled her in a skills-empowerment program where she learned tailoring. With a sewing machine provided through the empowerment grant, she now runs a small business and cares for her family independently. “My life ended the day I lost my husband,” Naomi says, “but it started again when I learned I could stand on my own.”
