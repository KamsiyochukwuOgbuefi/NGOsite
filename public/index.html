About The Crisis

Across Nigeria, Christian communities continue to endure a severe and deeply under-reported humanitarian crisis. Terrorist groups such as Boko Haram, ISWAP, and armed extremist militias routinely target Christian villages with killings, abductions, church burnings, and the destruction of homes and farmlands. Families in Chibok, Gwoza, Bama, Southern Kaduna, Plateau, and Benue have been forced to flee repeated waves of violence, often escaping with nothing.

Impact Statistics

Here are some of the most recent and alarming stats on Christian persecution killings in Nigeria:
• In the first 220 days of 2025, ~7,087 Christians were reportedly killed. (ACI Africa)
• In that same period, 7,800 Christians were violently abducted. (www.christian.com)
• That works out to an average of 30 Christian deaths per day. (ACI Africa)
• In 2024, about 3,100 Christians were killed and 2,830 kidnapped. (ACI Africa)
• Since 2009, one report suggests around 185,000 Nigerians have died in violence; of those, 125,000 were Christians. (ZENIT - English)

Survivor Stories

Hear from survivors whose lives has been touched by your generousity

Amara was only fourteen when her village was struck by a violent terrorist attack. In the chaos, she was separated from her family and left with injuries that would take months to heal. Yet today, she stands as a symbol of quiet strength—rebuilding her life through counseling, education support, and a community of survivors who refuse to give up hope. Her journey reminds us that behind every crisis is a resilient human spirit.

-Amara 12 Years Old

Grace escaped a violent attack, but the emotional wounds remained long after the chaos ended. She battled anxiety, nightmares, and a constant sense of fear that affected her daily life and education. Through trauma counseling, peer support groups, and mental-health resources provided by the NGO, she gradually learned to regain control and trust again. Today, Grace advocates for others to seek help

-Grace 26 Years Old

Daniel survived a violent attack that destroyed his home, farm, and sense of security. Forced to flee with his young family, he struggled to provide even basic needs. Through emergency relief, counseling, and a skills-training program, he slowly rebuilt stability and hope. Today, Daniel earns income as a carpenter and supports other displaced families. His story continues to inspire many survivors.

-Daniel, 23 Years Old
